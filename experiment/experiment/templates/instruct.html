You are about to play a game involving lists of numbers like the ones below:

Every list in this game has somewhere from 0 to 15 numbers in it. Each number is somewhere from 0 to 99.

The game is a guessing game divided into 5 rounds.

At the start of each round, the computer will think of a rule for transforming an input list into an output list. For example:

  • Rule: Count how many times 7 appears in the list.
  • Rule: Add 1 to each number.
  • Rule: Remove the numbers greater than 17.

After thinking of a rule, the computer will show you an input list. The game is to guess the rule and use it to guess the correct output.

You won't have much to go on at first, but after you guess, the correct answer will show up on screen, a new input will appear, and you'll get to guess again.

After a few guesses, your screen might look like this:

You will get to guess 10 times in each round. At the end of each round, you will be asked to describe in words what you think the rule was for that round.

To complete the HIT, you will:

  1. play 5 rounds of the guessing game with 10 guesses per round (25min)
  2. complete a short survey (5min)

Here are a few tips:

  • The rule the computer is thinking of is never random, rigged, or deceptive.
  • In each round, there is always one correct output for each input.
  • You'll advance immediately if you answer correctly, but will have to wait a few seconds if you answer incorrectly. Do your best!
  • Each round has a different rule, but the rule is always the same for an entire round, so past questions may help you answer future questions.

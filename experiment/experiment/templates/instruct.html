You are about to play a game involving lists of numbers.

Each list has 0 to 15 numbers:

Each number is from 0 to 99:

The game is a guessing game divided into rounds. In each round, the computer will think of a rule for transforming an input list into an output list.

For example:

  • Rule: Count how many times 7 appears in the list.
  • Rule: Add 1 to each number.
  • Rule: Remove the numbers greater than 17.

You won't know the rule at first, but the computer will ask you a series of questions like this one:

Your job is to guess the computer's rule and correctly answer as many questions as possible.

You can type in lists without or without square brackets, separating numbers with commas, spaces, or both. For example (you'd type what's between quotes):

"[1, 2, 3]"
"1,2,3"
"[1 2 3]"
"1 2 3"
"[1]"
"1"
"[]"
""

After you answer each question, the computer will show the correct answer and give you a new question. Your screen might eventually look like this:

Round 1

The computer has thought of a new rule. Can you figure it out?
Question 1: Incorrect. You said [75], but the correct answer is below.
Question 2: Incorrect. You said [54,75,39,19], but the correct answer is below.
Question 3: Correct!
Question 4: Incorrect. You said [80,80], but the correct answer is below.
Question 5:

At the end of each round, you will also be asked to describe in words what you think the rule was for that round. That will look like this:

You answered the last question. What do you think the computer's rule was?

To complete the HIT, you will:

  1. play 5 rounds of the guessing game (25min)
    • each round contains 11 questions
    • you end a round by describing the computer's rule in words
  2. complete a short survey (5min)

Here are a few tips:

  • The computer's rule is never random, rigged, or deceptive.
  • In each round, there is always one correct answer to each question.
  • You'll advance immediately if you answer correctly but will have to wait a few seconds if you answer incorrectly. Do your best!
  • Each correct response increases the bonus you will be paid after finishing the HIT.
  • Each round has a different rule, but the rule is always the same for an entire round, so past questions may help you answer future questions.
